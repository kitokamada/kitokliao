About

I am a physics master's student at CUNY/CCNY working toward a PhD in astrophysics. My research focuses on the CAMELS suite of cosmological simulations, exploring how astrophysical feedback processes shape galaxy formation across a wide parameter space. I work under the supervision of Francisco Villaescusa-Navarro at the Center for Computational Astrophysics at the Flatiron Institute.

My background is in electrical engineering (CCNY Grove School of Engineering), and I am on a self-directed path into astrophysics, with interests spanning machine learning for cosmology, galaxy formation physics, and simulation-based inference.

Research Interests

Cosmological simulations — galaxy formation — SUBFIND halo/subhalo catalogs — machine learning for astrophysics — simulation-based inference — astrophysical feedback
